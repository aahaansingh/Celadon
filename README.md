# Celadon
Celadon is an RSS desktop app for Mac, developed with Tauri.
It is designed primarily for myself and it has the features that I find useful and nothing else.
It is inspired by Terry Godier's [Current](https://www.terrygodier.com/current) reader for mobile.

You can read more about what Celadon is and why I made it the way I did when I end up writing about it (coming soon!)

Bear in mind that Celadon is currently quite unstable and I plan on making some major (though not breaking) changes in the near future. If you're using it, PLEASE reach out to me with any bugs that you've encountered.

## Features
- **Offline**: The only reason you need the internet is to get feed updates. Everything else is on your computer.
- **Weighted Display**: All articles disappear eventually, but you choose how long that takes (between 6 hours and a week). Your blog feeds and newsfeeds are on an even playing field.
- **Superfeeds**: They're like folders but a single feed can have multiple!
- **Non-Compact UI**: Let your eyes relax after staring at an inbox all day.
- **In-App Display**: Read your articles in the app itself.
- **Commands**: Navigate your articles, feeds, and superfeeds with [slash commands](commands.md).
